Main Dish One:

Musakhan~pronounced moo-sah-khan or msakhan

Chicken Musakhan

The national dish of Palestine — roasted chicken over taboon bread with caramelized onions, sumac, and toasted nuts.

Prep
15 min
Cook
60 min
Total
1 hr 15 min
Serves
4
Cuisine
Palestinian
Method
Baking

Chicken Musakhan is the national dish of Palestine. It features seasoned roasted chicken placed over taboon bread that is covered with caramelized onions cooked in extra virgin olive oil and generously spiced with sumac.

The Arabic word musakhan translates to "heated" — a name that captures the essence of this warm, comforting dish. Traditionally made during the fall to celebrate the olive harvest season using freshly pressed oil, it is believed to have originated in Tulkarem and Jenin, two northern cities in Palestine.

Musakhan is meant to be eaten with your hands, tearing pieces of bread and scooping up the sumac-spiced caramelized onions soaked in olive oil — a shared experience perfect for family gatherings and celebrations.

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 4 chicken legs (or a whole chicken cut into 4–6 pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil (for the chicken)
  • ½ fresh lemon, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon sumac (for the chicken)
  • ½ teaspoon sea salt (for the chicken)

Caramelized Onions

  • 3 red onions, finely chopped
  • ½ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1–2 tablespoons sumac
  • ½ teaspoon sea salt (for the onions)
  • Black pepper, to taste

To Assemble

  • 4 medium flatbreads (taboon, naan, or any flatbread)
  • ¼ cup toasted pine nuts and almonds

Instructions

Roast the Chicken

  1. Rub the chicken with olive oil, lemon juice, spices, sumac, and salt. Mix well to coat evenly.
  2. Place in a roasting pan skin side up.
  3. Roast in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 50–60 minutes.
  4. For extra color, place under the broiler for 2–4 minutes at the end.

Caramelize the Onions

  1. Finely chop the red onions.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium-high heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until soft and caramelized — about 10–15 minutes.
  3. Season with salt, black pepper, and sumac. Stir to combine.

Assemble and Serve

  1. Spoon a generous layer of caramelized onions over each flatbread.
  2. Place the bread under the broiler for 2–3 minutes until warmed and slightly crisped.
  3. Place a piece of roasted chicken over each bread.
  4. Sprinkle with toasted pine nuts and almonds.
  5. Serve immediately — eat with your hands, tearing the bread and scooping up the onions.

Substitutions & Variations

  • Sweet or yellow onions can be used in place of red onions, though red onions are less sweet and preferred for this dish.
  • Boneless chicken breast works — slice it after baking and layer over the bread.
  • Cornish hen can be used; cut each in half before roasting.
  • For a vegetarian version, use grilled portabella mushrooms or eggplant with olive oil and sumac in place of the chicken.

Expert Tips

  • Marinate the chicken with spices overnight in the fridge for deeper flavor.
  • To cut onions without tears: chop next to an open flame, or chill the onions in the freezer for 30 minutes first.
  • Toast pine nuts under a broiler for 2–4 minutes, or sauté in olive oil in a skillet for 5–7 minutes, stirring frequently.
  • Use a good quality extra virgin olive oil — it makes a significant difference in the final flavor of the onions.
