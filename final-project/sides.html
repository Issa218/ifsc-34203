Side Dish One:

Hummus~pronounced huh-muhs

Hummus حمص

Authentic Palestinian hummus — creamy, lemony, and far better than anything from a store.

Prep
10 min
Cook
0 min
Total
10 min
Serves
4
Cuisine
Palestinian
Method
Blending

Hummus is one of those dishes eaten on every occasion in Palestine — at a barbecue, alongside a falafel sandwich, or simply as a snack. It is a staple appetizer (mezza) and a dish that connects people around the table.

The authentic taste of hummus made at home differs noticeably from store-bought versions. Yet it is remarkably simple to prepare. Canned chickpeas work well when you're short on time — just rinse them thoroughly. If you have more time, dried chickpeas soaked overnight and cooked fresh produce an even creamier result.

Taste as you go before serving — everyone has their preference. Start with less lemon and salt, then adjust. A good hummus should be smooth, creamy, and just right for dipping.

Ingredients

Base

  • 1 can chickpeas (garbanzo beans), drained and rinsed well
  • ¼ cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 large lemon, freshly squeezed
  • 1 clove garlic
  • Salt, to taste

Optional & To Serve

  • 1 jalapeño (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • Fresh parsley, for garnishing
  • Drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, to serve
  • Pita bread or fresh vegetables, for dipping

Instructions

Make the Hummus

  1. Drain and rinse the canned chickpeas thoroughly under cold water.
  2. Add the chickpeas, tahini, water, lemon juice, garlic, and salt to a food processor.
  3. If using, add the jalapeño now.
  4. Blend until completely smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust lemon or salt as needed.
  5. Transfer to a serving bowl, drizzle with olive oil, and garnish with parsley.
  6. Serve with pita bread or fresh vegetables for dipping.

Substitutions & Variations

  • For a creamier texture, use dried chickpeas soaked overnight in water, then boiled until very tender before blending.
  • Add a jalapeño into the blend for a spicy version — it pairs beautifully with the lemon and tahini.
  • A pinch of cumin blended in adds a warm, earthy depth.
  • For extra smoothness, peel the chickpeas before blending — it takes a few minutes but makes a noticeably silkier hummus.

Expert Tips

  • Rinse canned chickpeas very well to remove the liquid from the can, which can affect flavor.
  • Start with less lemon and salt than you think you need — taste first, then adjust to your preference.
  • If the hummus is too thick, add water a tablespoon at a time while blending until you reach the right consistency.
  • Blend for longer than you think necessary — a full 2–3 minutes in the food processor gives a much creamier result.
  • Serve at room temperature for the best flavor; cold hummus straight from the fridge dulls the taste.
